BLIND SPOT DETECTION AND LANE DEPARTURE DETECTION SYSTEM SWITCH

The BSD and LDD systems can be permanently activated or deactivated through the [DRIVER ASSISTANCE] menu:

1. Press the <SETUP> button on the NISSAN CONNECT unit.

2. Touch [SYSTEM] on the display.

3. Touch [DRIVER ASSISTANCE].

4. Touch [Blind Spot Detection] or [Lane Departure Detection] to switch eithersystem [ON] or [OFF].

To temporarily deactivate both the BSD and LDD systems, press the warning systems switch on the lower instrument panel. The systems will automatically be reactivated next time the ignition is switched ON.

Blind Spot Detection

The Blind Spot Detection (BSD) system operates at speeds over 32 km/h (20 mph) and detects vehicles in the blind spots alongside your vehicle.

If the BSD system is activated through the [DRIVER ASSISTANCE] menu and the ignition is switched ON, the BSD indicator light will light up in orange after which it will turn green to show that the system is correctly functioning.

Lane Departure Detection

The Lane Departure Detection (LDD) system operates at speeds over approximately 60 km/h (37 mph) and alerts you when you approach either the left or right lane markers.

If the LDD system is activated through the [DRIVER ASSISTANCE] menu and the ignition is switched ON, the LDD indicator light will light up in orange after which it will turn green to show that the system is correctly functioning.
